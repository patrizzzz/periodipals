Menstruation

Days 1-7

Your body sheds the uterine lining that built up during the previous cycle. This is both the end and start of your cycle!

Self-care tips:

Use pads or tampons, stay hydrated, and rest when needed. Light exercise can help with cramps.

Follicular Phase

Days 1-13

Your body prepares for possible pregnancy by developing follicles in the ovaries. Estrogen levels begin to rise.

Self-care tips:

This is a great time for physical activity as energy levels typically increase with rising estrogen.

Ovulation

Days 13-15

A mature egg is released from the ovary. This is the most fertile time when pregnancy is most likely to occur.

Self-care tips:

Some people experience mild pain on one side during ovulation. This is normal and called mittelschmerz.

Luteal Phase

Days 15-28

Your body prepares the uterus lining for a possible pregnancy. If pregnancy doesn't occur, the cycle starts again.

Self-care tips:

PMS symptoms may occur during this phase. Practice self-care and maintain a healthy diet.

Taking Care of Yourself

Track Your Cycle

Use a calendar or app to note when your period starts. This helps you predict when to expect it next time.

Be Prepared

Keep period supplies in your backpack just in case. You never know when your period might start!

Listen to Your Body

It's normal to feel tired or have cramps. Rest when you need to, and use a warm compress if you have cramps.

Talk to Someone

If you have questions or concerns, talk to a trusted adult - a parent, school nurse, or doctor.
